Coaches and Staff

Desireé Natasha Fox - Director, Coach, Choreographer and Co-founder

Born and raised in NY, Desiree’s been dancing ever since she could walk. She received a B.A. in Dance with an emphasis in ballroom dance from Brigham Young University in 2006. In 2007 she received her advance teaching certification from the International Society of Teachers of Dancing. During her amateur career (2002-2006), Desireé achieved 10 and 9 dance status competing Amateur Standard, Latin, Rhythm, and Smooth. Her achievements include many regional and National titles including National Smooth Finalist 2005. In 2005, she started coaching pre-teen, junior, youth formation teams and competitive couples in Utah. Year after year her teams won National Championships and acquired many National Titles. Desireé met her husband Charles at BYU. They competed in Amateur Latin for two years. They were married in November 2006 and Desireé turned Professional shortly after. They have two sons Evan who is 2, and Asher who is 8 months old. Both boys love to dance.

Since moving to California over 6 years ago, Desireé has established herself by creating and directing the Bay Area Elite Dance Company in 2008 and opening Elite Ballroom Studio in 2011. In addition to full time wife and mother, she works as CEO of Elite Ballroom, Artistic Director and Choreographer for the BAE Dance Company, and as a private ballroom dance coach for multiple couples in the Bay Area. She commutes all over the country for multiple groups as their guest choreographer and continues to coach youth formation teams all over the country. In 2008-2009 she was the co-creator and co-executive director of a non-profit organization that teaches dance and etiquette to underprivileged elementary children in South San-Jose. From 2010 - 2013 Desiree' was appointed by the Mayor of Mountain View to complete 3 terms of service on the Performing Arts Committee which works to enrich the community with Perfoming Arts and Cultural oppurtunities.

Recently her achievements reside as the director of the Bay Area Elite Amateur Adult Formation Team who in addition to maintaining CA state and Western US Championship status for 5 years, was also invited to represent the USA in the World Championships in Blackpool England May 2012. During the 2012 season Desiree' and her team of engineers created and debuted the first of it's kind Ballroom Dance Costumes with EL lights. The lights on the costumes changed throughout the routine powered by a program the Elite engineers wrote themselves. It was such an impressive project many other popular programs such as BYU's Ballroom Dance Program were inspired to do the same following the BAE's success.
