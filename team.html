Coaches and Staff

Desireé Natasha Fox - Director, Coach, Choreographer and Co-founder

Born and raised in NY, Desiree’s been dancing ever since she could walk. She received a B.A. in Dance with an emphasis in ballroom dance from BYU in 2006. In 2007 she received her advance teaching certification from the International Society of Teachers of Dancing. During her amateur career (2002-2006), Desireé achieved 10 and 9 dance status competing Amateur Standard, Latin, Rhythm, and Smooth. Her achievements include many regional and National titles including National Smooth Finalist 2005. In 2005, she started coaching pre-teen, junior, youth formation teams and competitive couples in Utah. Year after year her teams won National Championships and acquired many National Titles. Desireé met her husband Charles at BYU. They competed in Amateur Latin for two years. They were married in November 2006 and Desireé turned Professional shortly after.

Since moving to California over a year ago, Desireé has established herself by creating and directing the Bay Area Elite Dance Company. In addition she works full time as a private ballroom dance coach for various studios in the Bay Area. She commutes all over the country for multiple groups as their guest choreographer and continues to coach youth formation teams all over the country. In 2008-2009 she was the co-creator and co-executive director of a non-profit organization that teaches dance and etiquette to underprivileged elementary children in South San-Jose. In January 2010 Desiree' was appointed by the Mayor of Mountain View to complete a term of service on the Performing Arts Committee which works to enrich the community with Perfoming Arts and Cultural oppurtunities.

Recently her achievements reside as the director of the Bay Area Elite Amateur Adult Formation Team. Her coaching brought two regional first place victories at the 2009 San Francisco Open and the 2009 Emerald Ball, and three first place victories at the 2010 City Lights Ball, UC Berkeley Classic, and SFOpen. These victories claimed the titles of California State and Western US Champions.
